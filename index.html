Chocolate Fudge

Chocolate fudge can be made using a simple, stovetop method with three key ingredients.
You can also find more complex versions involving candy thermometers, or opt for an even quicker microwave version.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups semi-sweet or milk chocolate chips
  • 1 (14-ounce) can sweetened condensed milk
  • 1/2 cup salted butter

Steps

  1. Prepare the pan: line an 8x8 inch baking pan with parchment paper, leaving some overhand on the sides. Lightly coat the paper with nonstick spray.
  2. Combine ingredients: In a small saucepan, combine the chocolate ships, sweetened condensed milk, and butter.
  3. Melt the mixture: Cook over low heart, stirring consistantly, for 5-7 minutes until the chocolate melts and the mixture becomes smooth and glossy. Avoid overheating the chocolate, which can cause it to seize.
  4. Set the fudge: Pour the mixture into the prepared pan and smooth the top evently with a spatula.
  5. Chill: Allow the fudge to set at room temperature for about 4 hours, or in the refrigerator for about 2 hours, until firm.
  6. Cut and serve: Use the parchment paper overhang to lift the fudge out of the pan. Cut into squares with a sharp knife and store in an airtight container.
